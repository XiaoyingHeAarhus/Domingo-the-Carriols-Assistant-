Primary reference · Book

Creoles, Pidgins, and Mixed Languages — Ross & Mous (eds.)

The key reference volume for this project. Covers typology, genesis, and structural properties of creole and pidgin languages, with substantial coverage of Dutch-lexifier creoles including Negerhollands. Chapters address phonology, morphosyntax, TMA systems, and the role of substrate and superstrate languages. This is the core theoretical and descriptive foundation for Domingo's knowledge of Carriols grammar and lexicon.

Primary corpus · MPI Language Archive

NEHOL — Negerhollands Corpus (MPI Nijmegen)

A digitised corpus of historical Negerhollands texts spanning 1742–1936, hosted by the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics. Contains 43 bundles across four subcorpora:
  • Basilectal data — language as spoken by the enslaved population and their descendants. The De Josselin de Jong 1926 narratives (DJDJ texts) are the most voluminous source.
  • Missionary data — 18th-century texts from Moravian (German) and Danish Lutheran missions.
  • Colonists' data — variety spoken natively by locally born European-descent inhabitants.
  • Slave letters — letters written by or dictated by enslaved persons, aiming at acrolectal Negerhollands or Dutch.
Formats: PDF, XML, Toolbox text, plain text. 32 of 43 bundles are in Negerhollands. Access is predominantly open.

Key secondary literature

Selected Scholarly References

  • Hesseling, D.C. (1905). Het Negerhollands der Deense Antillen. Leiden: Sijthoff. — The foundational grammatical description of Negerhollands.
  • Van Name, A. (1869–70). Contributions to Creole Grammar. Transactions of the American Philological Association, 1, 123–167. — Earliest comparative description including Negerhollands.
  • De Josselin de Jong, J.P.B. (1926). Het huidige Negerhollandsch. Amsterdam. — Primary source for basilectal 20th-century data.
  • Sabino, R. (2012). Language Contact in the Danish West Indies. Brill. — Sociolinguistic history of Negerhollands and its ecology.
  • Stolz, T. (1986). Gibt es das kreolische Sprachwandelmodell? Frankfurt: Lang. — Theoretical treatment of creole change including Dutch creoles.
  • Bakker, P. et al. (2011). Dutch-lexifier contact languages. In Creoles, their substrates, and language typology. Amsterdam: Benjamins.
  • Magens, J.M. (1770). Grammatica over det Creolske Sprog. Copenhagen. — First grammar of Negerhollands, by a native speaker.
